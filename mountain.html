Mount Everest Summit

Atmospheric Study No. 0001

The Description

Mount Everest is Earth's highest mountain above sea level, reaching an elevation of 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet). Located in the Mahalangur Himal sub-range of the Himalayas, its summit straddles the border between Nepal and the Tibet Autonomous Region of China. Known as Sagarmatha in Nepali and Chomolungma in Tibetan, it is a premier, challenging destination for mountaineers.

History

The Himalayas were created roughly 50 million years ago when the Indian tectonic plate collided with the Eurasian plate. The mountain is a major, yet dangerous, destination for mountaineers, with increasing commercialization raising concerns about safety and overcrowding.
